Spiritual

One can find temples in every nook and corner of Andhra Pradesh. These sacred temples in Andhra Pradesh not only has great religious significance but also has a brilliant centuries-old architecture. Among these mystical temples, Tirupati Balaji temple is the most visited temple in Andhra Pradesh. Also, Badrachalam Sitaramswamy Temple, Kanaka Durga temple, Srisailam temple are few of the temples that must-visit temples in Andhra Pradesh.

Tirumala

Tirumala Venkateswara Temple is located in Tirupati district of Andhra Pradesh.

Main deity

Sri Venkateswara (Lord Vishnu)

History

The history of Tirumala Temple dates back almost 2,000 years. It is mentioned in Sangam poetry and is believed to have been consecrated by a queen from the Pallava dynasty. Lord Sri Venkateswara made Tirumala his abode 5,000 years ago, and even before him, Lord Varahaswami resided there.

Nearest airport

Tirupati


Sri Kalahasteeswara Temple

The Srikalahasti Temple is located in the town of Srikalahasti, in Tirupati district in the state of Andhra Pradesh.

Main deity

Lord Shiva

History

The temple is also regarded as Rahu-Ketu kshetra and Dakshina Kailasam. Srikalahasti temple, situated 36 km away from Tirupati, is famous for its Vayu Lingam (Wind Lingam), one of the Pancha Bhuta Sthalams, representing the wind.

Nearest airport

Tirupati

Sri Durga Malleswara Swamy

Kanaka Durga Temple, officially known as Sri Durga Malleswara Swamyvarla Devasthanam, is a Hindu temple dedicated to Kanaka Durga. The deity in this temple is also popularly referred as Kanaka Durga. The temple is located in Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh.

Main deity

Durga

History

The popular legend is about the triumph of Kanaka Durga over the demon King Mahishasura. It is said that the growing menace of demons became unbearable for the natives. Sage Indrakila practiced severe penance, and when the goddess appeared the sage pleaded to her to reside on his head and keep a vigil on the wicked demons. As per his wishes of killing the demons, Durga made Indrakila her permanent abode. Later, she also slayed the demon king Mahishasura freeing the people of Vijayawada from evil.

Nearest airport

Vijayawada

Lepakshi Temple

Veerabhadra temple is a Hindu temple located in the Lepakshi, in the state of Andhra Pradesh.

Main deity

Veerabhadra

History

Built in the 16th century, the architectural features of the temple are in the Vijayanagara style with profusion of carvings and paintings at almost every exposed surface of the temple. It is one of the centrally protected monuments of national importance and is considered one of the most spectacular Vijayanagara temples. The fresco paintings are particularly detailed in very bright dresses and colours with scenes of Rama and Krishna from the epic stories of the Ramayana, the Mahabharata and the Puranas and they are well preserved.

Nearest airport

Puttaparthi

Yaganti Temple

Yaganti Temple or Sri Yaganti Uma Maheswara Temple is a temple of Shiva in Nandyal district in the India state of Andhra Pradesh.

Main deity

Lord Shiva

History

This temple was constructed by King Harihara Bukka Raya of the Sangama Dynasty of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 15th century. It was built according to Vaishnavaite traditions.

Nearest airport

Kurnool
