The history of Karnataka spans several millennia. It has been home to various cultures and civilizations, including the Mauryas, Chalukyas, Rashtrakutas, Vijayanagar Empire, and Hoysalas. These dynasties left lasting cultural and architectural legacies, shaping Karnataka’s rich heritage.
Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, India, was once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire. Its sprawling ruins, spread across 4,100 hectares, include over 1,600 structures—temples, forts, and markets. Hampi’s grand architecture, set against the Tungabhadra River, offers a captivating glimpse into South India’s glorious past.
Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical royal residence located in Mysore, Karnataka, India. It used to be the official home of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore. The palace, a three-story gray granite structure with Indo-Saracenic architecture, stands within a historic fort. Its construction was overseen by British architect Henry Irwin, and it was completed in 1912.
Badami, formerly known as Vātāpi, is an ancient town in the Bagalkot district of Karnataka, India. It served as the regal capital of the Badami Chalukyas from 540 to 757. Nestled at the foot of rugged red sandstone cliffs, Badami boasts remarkable rock-cut monuments like the Badami cave temples and structural temples such as the Bhutanatha temples, Badami Shivalaya, and Jambulingesvara Temple. The Agastya lake, a man-made water source, adds to its historical allure.
